Figure 4 — Chronological Timeline of the Three-Phase Campaign, 1591–1605
1591
Twenty-nine attacks on Gdańsk merchant ships in a single month.
Origin of the casus belli. Sigismund III's first letter to Elizabeth.
1595
Sigismund III's second letter to Elizabeth I.
No substantive reply received. Diplomatic exhaustion confirmed.
1597 — February–March
Polish Sejm convenes at Warsaw. Caietani and Mendoza both present. Działyński appointed ambassador.
Convergence of Polish, Spanish, and papal interests.
1597 — August 4
Phase One. Greenwich audience. Działyński delivers oration in Latin; withholds title Defensor Fidei. Elizabeth's extemporaneous reply.
Mercurius Sarmaticus circulates to Rome and Madrid.
1597 — July–August
Phase Two. Isle of Dogs performed at Swan Theatre by Pembroke's Men. Jonson arrested; Nashe flees. Poley imprisoned with Jonson.
Topcliffe reports to Cecil. All London theatres closed.
1598
Death of Lord Burghley (August 4). Power vacuum at centre of Elizabethan government.
Rational Catholic expectation: succession crisis now imminent.
1601 — February
Essex rebellion fails. Essex executed. Catesby fined four thousand marks. Robert Cecil emerges dominant.
Second pathway to Catholic-friendly succession closed.
1603 — March
Death of Elizabeth I. Smooth transition to James VI (James I), managed in secret by Cecil.
Catholic expectation of contested succession confounded.
1603 — Summer
Wintour travels to Spain. Catesby seeks support from Philip III. Promises made; none fulfilled.
Continuity of Spanish connection from 1597 confirmed.
1604
James I's first parliament renews recusancy fines; rejects toleration.
Final closure of political space. Plot conceived.
1605 — November 5
Phase Three. Gunpowder Plot discovered. Conspirators (Catesby, Wintour, Wright) arrested or killed. James I survives.
Terminal point of the campaign. Strategic bankruptcy confirmed.
1606 onward
Jacobean persecutions. Penal laws tightened. Catholic priests expelled. Catholicism permanently excluded as political force.
The campaign created precisely the outcome it sought to prevent.

Fig. 4. Chronological timeline of the three-phase campaign. Phase One nodes (red marker) = Polish-diplomatic. Phase Two nodes (blue marker) = Pembroke-theatrical. Phase Three nodes (gold marker) = Gunpowder. Bridge nodes (green, filled) = structural transitions between phases.
